Evaluating Backbone Scaling and Attention Mechanisms with Explainability in Pneumonia Detection

"This work compares backbone scaling (EfficientNet), attention mechanisms (CBAM), and explanation methods (Grad-CAM vs SHAP) to evaluate both performance and interpretability in medical AI."

Problem Statement

Deep learning models achieve high performance in pneumonia detection, yet their internal reasoning remains opaque. This study investigates whether architectural scaling outperforms attention augmentation, and evaluates explanation techniques to understand model decision-making behavior.

Literature Review

Rajpurkar et al. (2017) introduced CheXNet using DenseNet for pneumonia detection. Tan & Le (2019) proposed EfficientNet compound scaling. Woo et al. (2018) introduced CBAM attention. Selvaraju et al. (2017) proposed Grad-CAM. Lundberg & Lee (2017) introduced SHAP for model explainability.

Research Contribution

We combine architectural comparison with quantitative explainability metrics (AOPC, Sparsity, Probability Drop) to analyze both accuracy and reasoning stability.

System Methodology

Dataset

RSNA Pneumonia Detection Dataset (30k+ images).

Models Compared

DenseNet121, DenseNet+CBAM, EfficientNetB3, EfficientNetB4.

Explainability

Grad-CAM and SHAP evaluated quantitatively.
